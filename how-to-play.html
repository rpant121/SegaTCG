HOW TO PLAY

SEGA CARD GAME TCG — RULES REFERENCE

OVERVIEW

Each player builds a 30-card deck and selects a Leader. Players take turns playing cards, using abilities, and attacking until one Leader's HP reaches zero.

DECK RULES

  • Exactly 30 cards per deck
  • Max 3 copies of any single Unit card
  • Max 4 copies of any single Equipment or Stage card
  • Max 1 Genesis card per deck (0 is legal)
  • Your Leader is NOT part of the 30 — it is placed separately
  • Cards are filtered by your chosen Leader's IP (Sonic, Persona 5, etc.)

WIN CONDITION

  • Reduce your opponent's Leader HP to 0 or below
  • Check after every damage event
  • If both Leaders reach 0 on the same action, the active player wins

SETUP

  1. Each player shuffles their 30-card deck
  2. Each player draws an opening hand of 6 cards
  3. During the Setup Phase, each player deploys up to 3 Support Units from their hand to their bench (free, no cost)
  4. A coin flip determines who goes first
  5. The first player does not draw on their very first turn

TURN STRUCTURE

Every turn follows these phases in order:

  1. DRAW Draw 1 card. Some Stage cards or unit passives grant additional draws. If your deck is empty, take escalating damage instead (10 on 1st miss, 20 on 2nd, etc.). A successful draw resets the miss counter. Note: certain bench unit passives trigger at the very start of your turn before you draw.
  2. ENERGY Your maximum Energy increases by 1, then your current Energy is fully restored to that new maximum. Some Stage cards grant a bonus +1 Energy on top of this. Unspent Energy does NOT carry over between turns.
  3. MAIN Play cards, use Leader actives, and use bench unit actives in any order and any number of times while you can afford them. See Main Phase Actions below.
  4. ATTACK Your Leader attacks. Choose a target: the opponent's Leader, or one of their bench Support Units. See Combat below.
  5. END Tails draws 1 card (if not exhausted). All your exhausted units recover. Temporary buffs (Chaos Emerald, Power Glove, Master Emerald) clear. Turn passes to your opponent.

MAIN PHASE ACTIONS

PLAYING A CARD

Click any card in your hand to play it. Pay its Energy cost. Support Units have no deployment cost — just click to place them on your bench (max 3 units).

Each Leader has a unique active ability usable during the Main Phase. Cost varies by Leader.

UNIT ACTIVES

Click a bench unit to use its active ability. The unit becomes exhausted and loses its passive until End Phase. While Master Emerald is active, all unit actives cost 0 and units do not exhaust.

COMBAT

EFFECTIVE DAMAGE

Your Leader's damage is calculated fresh before every attack:

Effective Damage = Leader base damage + Chaos Emerald buff + Power Glove buff + sum of all non-exhausted attack boost passives on your bench

ATTACKING THE OPPONENT'S LEADER

When you target the opponent's Leader, they may choose to intercept with one of their non-exhausted bench units.

NEW Intercepting — The opponent sends a bench unit to stand in front of the attack. The unit absorbs the full damage. If the interceptor is KO'd, overflow damage spills to the Leader. Overflow is at minimum 20, or more if the attack exceeded the unit's remaining HP.

Example: Sonic deals 40 damage. Knuckles (30 HP) intercepts. Knuckles is KO'd with 10 excess. Overflow = max(20, 40−30) = 20 damage to the Leader.

Example: Sonic deals 60 damage. Knuckles (20 HP) intercepts. Knuckles is KO'd with 40 excess. Overflow = max(20, 60−20) = 40 damage to the Leader.

Targeting a Bench Unit directly does not overflow. If you choose to attack a unit and KO it, no damage reaches the Leader. The opponent cannot intercept a direct unit attack.

DAMAGE REDUCTION (Defending Leader)

Non-exhausted bench units with damage reduction passives (e.g. Cream, Tae Takumi, Sojiro Sakura) reduce incoming damage to the Leader. These stack additively. Reduction applies after intercept overflow is calculated.

UNBLOCKABLE ATTACKS

Certain effects (e.g. Sae Niijima's active) make the next Leader attack uninterceptable. The opponent cannot send a unit to intercept — the attack hits the Leader directly.

CARD TYPES

UNIT

SUPPORT UNITS

  • Deploy to bench during Main Phase — no Energy cost
  • Max 3 units on bench at once
  • Passives are always active while not exhausted
  • Actives cost Energy and exhaust the unit
  • Exhausted units recover at End Phase
  • KO'd units go to discard (and may trigger overflow if intercepting)
EQUIPMENT

EQUIPMENT CARDS

  • Play from hand during Main Phase, pay Energy cost
  • Effect resolves immediately, then the card goes to discard
  • Examples: Ring (+1 Energy), Chaos Emerald (+20 damage), Chili Dog (heal 20 HP)
STAGE

STAGE CARDS

  • Play from hand, pay Energy cost
  • Only 1 Stage active at a time — playing a new one sends the old one to your discard
  • Effects persist until replaced
  • Examples: Green Hill Zone (both draw +1/turn), Radical Highway (+1 Energy/turn)
GENESIS

GENESIS CARDS

  • Max 1 Genesis card per deck
  • Play from hand during Main Phase, pay Energy cost
  • Powerful one-time effects
  • Examples: Master Emerald (all unit actives free this turn), Arsène Unleashed (halve a leader's HP)

ENERGY

  • You gain 1 max Energy per turn (no cap)
  • Each turn you refresh to your full max Energy
  • Unspent Energy does not carry over
  • Ring: +1 Energy immediately (can spend same turn)
  • Speed Shoes: +3 Energy immediately
  • Extreme Gear: Discard up to 3 cards, +1 Energy per discard
  • Radical Highway (Stage): +1 Energy to the active player each turn
  • Palace Infiltration Route (Stage): +1 Energy to the active player each turn, but both leaders take 10 damage

SPECIAL MECHANICS

EXHAUSTION

A unit becomes exhausted when it uses its active ability. Exhausted units: lose their passive bonus, cannot intercept, and cannot use their active. They recover at the End Phase of the controlling player's turn.

DECK-OUT DAMAGE

If your deck is empty during Draw Phase, instead of drawing you take damage equal to 10 × number of consecutive failed draws. (10 on 1st miss, 20 on 2nd, 30 on 3rd, etc.) A successful draw resets the counter to 0.

TAUNT

Some units (e.g. Sojiro Sakura) can gain Taunt. While a unit has Taunt, the opponent must choose it as the intercept target if they intercept — they cannot send a different unit. Taunt expires at the start of your next turn.
