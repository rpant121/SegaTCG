Rules & Reference

How to Play Sega Card Game

A two-player card game built around resource management, board presence, and knowing when to swing. Games typically last twenty to forty minutes.

The basics

What you're trying to do

Each player controls Sonic the Hedgehog as their Leader. Your Leader has ten hit points. Reduce your opponent's Leader to zero and you win.

You do that by attacking — once per turn, automatically, during the Attack Phase. The question is always whether to aim at their Leader directly or chip away at their bench first. A well-built bench makes your Leader hit harder, absorb damage, and generate card advantage. Tearing it down before going for the Leader is often the smarter play.

Win Condition

A player loses the moment their Leader's HP reaches zero or below. This is checked immediately after every damage event — mid-turn wins are possible.

Card Types

The four kinds of cards

Every card in your deck belongs to one of four types. Each behaves differently and costs energy differently.

Support Unit

Characters you deploy to your bench. They have HP and two abilities: a passive that runs continuously while they're not exhausted, and an active you can trigger by spending energy. Free to deploy — no energy cost.

Equipment

One-shot effects you play from your hand. Most give energy, deal damage, or buff your Leader for the turn. Costs energy to play. Goes to discard after use.

Stage

Persistent effects that stay active on the board until replaced. Only one Stage can be active at a time. Playing a new Stage sends the old one to your discard.

Genesis

Powerful one-time effects. You can only include one Genesis card in your entire deck — these are the big swings that can shift a game's momentum.

Before you play

Setup

Both players build a thirty-card deck using the deck builder, then take turns at the setup screen. Your Leader — Sonic the Hedgehog — is placed in the Leader zone and is not part of your thirty cards.

Once both decks are ready and the game launches, each player draws five cards as their opening hand. A coin flip determines who goes first. The first player skips their Draw Phase on turn one — a small compensation for the tempo advantage of moving first.

Deck Rules

Exactly thirty cards per deck. Maximum three copies of any individual non-Genesis card. Maximum one Genesis card total (zero is fine). The deck builder enforces all of this automatically.

Each turn

Turn structure

Every turn runs through the same sequence of phases. Some happen automatically; others require a decision from you.

  1. 00

    Recovery

    All exhausted support units on your bench recover and become available again. This happens before anything else, so units you used last turn are ready to go.

  2. 01

    Big Scry

    If Big is on your bench and not exhausted, you peek at the top card of your deck. You can either discard it or leave it in place. This phase is skipped if your deck is empty or Big is exhausted.

  3. 02

    Draw Phase

    Draw one card from the top of your deck. If Green Hill Zone is active, draw two instead. If your deck is empty, you take damage equal to the number of consecutive turns you have failed to draw — one on the first miss, two on the second, and so on. Drawing successfully resets this counter.

  4. 03

    Energy Phase

    Your maximum energy increases by one permanently, then your current energy refills to that new maximum. Energy does not carry over between turns — whatever you don't spend is lost. If Radical Highway is active, you gain one extra energy this phase.

  5. 04

    Main Phase

    The bulk of each turn. You may do any of the following in any order, as many times as you can afford: play cards from your hand, deploy support units to your bench (free), activate Sonic's leader ability (once per turn), or trigger bench unit actives. Each unit active can only be used once per turn — using one exhausts the unit, disabling its passive until your next recovery phase.

  6. 05

    Attack Phase

    Your Leader attacks. You choose the target: the opponent's Leader, or one of their bench units. Damage is calculated, applied, and the phase ends. You cannot skip attacking, but you can choose to end the phase without selecting a target.

  7. 06

    End Phase

    Tails draws a card if she is on your bench and not exhausted. All turn-based buffs — Chaos Emerald, Power Glove, Master Emerald — are cleared. The pass screen appears and the device passes to your opponent.

Resources

Energy

Energy is the currency for everything you do. On turn one each player has one maximum energy, on turn two they have two, and so on — there is no cap. This means the game naturally accelerates as it goes on, with more expensive plays becoming available over time.

Energy is spent on playing Equipment, Stage, and Genesis cards; using Sonic's active ability; and triggering bench unit actives. Deploying a support unit from your hand to your bench is always free.

Ring & Speed Shoes

Ring gives one energy immediately when played. Speed Shoes gives three. Both can be spent within the same Main Phase they were played — the energy is available right away, not next turn.

Super Form

This Genesis card doubles your current energy. If you have six energy when you play it, you now have twelve. Combine with Speed Shoes and Radical Highway for game-winning energy turns.

Dealing damage

Combat

Your Leader's base damage is one. This goes up with passives and equipment. The formula is recalculated fresh before every attack and is never stored.

Effective Damage Calculation

Base damage (1, or 2 if Shadow is on your bench and not exhausted), plus Chaos Emerald buff, plus Power Glove buff, plus one for each non-exhausted bench unit with an attack-boost passive (Knuckles, Amy).

When your Leader attacks an opponent's bench unit, the unit takes the full effective damage with no reduction. If the unit's HP reaches zero, it is KO'd — sent to the discard pile — and its controller takes two damage to their Leader as a penalty. This penalty is bypassed if Midnight Carnival is the active Stage.

When attacking the opponent's Leader directly, any non-exhausted Cream on the defending side reduces the incoming damage by one per Cream. Multiple damage-reduction passives stack.

Elemental Shield

Elemental Shield prevents all damage to yourself during your opponent's next turn — including the KO penalty from your own bench units dying. It triggers on the first damage event and then clears, so it only protects for one full turn.

Shadow's Self-Damage

Shadow's active deals three damage to the opponent's Leader but costs your Leader two unblockable damage in return. This damage ignores shields, Cream reduction, and blocking. It cannot be prevented by any means.

Defence

Blocking

When the attacking player targets your Leader, you have the option to intercept the attack with one of your non-exhausted bench units. A prompt will appear showing all eligible blockers and the incoming damage — you can see at a glance which units would survive and which would be KO'd.

If you choose to block, the full attack damage goes to the blocker instead of your Leader. The blocker does not exhaust from blocking. If the blocker is KO'd, the normal two-damage penalty still applies — blocking with a weak unit can therefore backfire.

Block Priority Rules

Elemental Shield takes effect before blocking is offered — if your shield is active, there is nothing to block. Blocking is only presented when you have at least one non-exhausted bench unit and the attacker has chosen your Leader as their target.

Cream and Blocking

Cream's damage-reduction passive applies to damage taken by your Leader specifically. If a bench unit is blocking instead of your Leader, Cream's passive does not reduce the blocker's incoming damage.

Construction

Building your deck

Decks are thirty cards. You can run up to three copies of any non-Genesis card, and exactly one Genesis card if you want one at all. The deck builder validates your list before you can start a game.

Three preconstructed decks are available if you want to jump straight into playing:

Team Dark — Aggro

Stack Knuckles and Amy for passive attack bonuses. Silver keeps them firing without exhausting. Shadow raises base damage to two. Midnight Carnival lets you attack aggressively without fearing KO blowback. Closes games with Mighty's second attack or a Power Glove burst.

Discard Engine — Setup

Big scrys expensive cards into the discard early. Tails replays them cheaply from discard for two energy instead of their print cost. Rouge draws a card every time something hits the discard, generating momentum. Blaze resets the loop when the discard gets thin. The target is to replay Master Emerald from discard for two energy, then chain all your unit actives for free.

Chaotix Burst — Rings

Charmy discounts chained equipment. Stack Ring, Speed Shoes, and Extreme Gear for massive energy generation. Espio recovers the hand after the burst turn. Vector converts total energy spent into direct damage — the combo turn where you spend fifteen energy often ends the game on the spot. Super Form is the nova button.

Strategy

A few things worth knowing

Bench pressure matters

A full bench with Knuckles and Amy active gives your Leader three extra damage. That is the difference between a four-turn clock and a two-turn clock. Targeting those units before they can be used is often better than going straight at the Leader.

The KO penalty cuts both ways

When your bench unit dies, you take two damage. That sounds bad, and it is — but Midnight Carnival negates it entirely. Running Midnight Carnival as your Stage and throwing your units into the line of fire can be a deliberate strategy, especially if Blaze is on the bench to heal it back.

Silver changes the math on actives

Normally using an active exhausts the unit and kills its passive for a full turn. With Silver on the bench, friendly units don't exhaust — Silver does instead. This means Knuckles and Amy keep their attack boosts active even after firing their actives. The combination is the core of the Aggro deck and is very difficult to race.

Don't forget Sonic's active

Discarding one card to draw two is a net gain of one card. Early in the game when you have dead cards in hand — equipment you can't afford yet, units when your bench is full — this is free value. It can only be used once per turn and requires at least one card in hand to activate.

Blocking is a tempo trade

Intercepting an attack with a bench unit saves your Leader's HP but risks losing the unit — and triggering the two-damage KO penalty if the blocker dies. Block with a unit that can survive, or block with one that has already used its active and will exhaust at the start of your next turn anyway, making it low-value to keep alive.
