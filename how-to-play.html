Rules Reference

How to Play
Sega Card Game

A two-player card game. Each player builds a thirty-card deck, places their Leader, and tries to reduce the opponent's Leader to zero HP. Games are designed to last around 6–10 minutes.

Before you start

Setup

Both players build their decks using the deck builder, then hand the device to each other in turn. Once both decks are saved, the game starts.

Each player draws 5 cards as their opening hand. A coin flip decides who goes first. The first player skips their Draw Phase on turn one — that's their only disadvantage for going first.

Your Leader starts in the Leader zone with full HP. They are not part of your thirty-card deck.

Each turn

Turn Structure

Every turn runs through these phases in order. Most happen automatically — you only need to make decisions during the Main Phase and Attack Phase.

  1. 1

    Recovery

    All exhausted support units on your bench recover and are ready to use again.

  2. 2

    Draw Phase

    Draw 1 card. Some units and stages may modify how many cards you draw. If your deck is empty, you take damage instead — 1 damage the first time, 2 the second, and so on. That counter resets when you successfully draw.

  3. 3

    Energy Phase

    Your maximum energy goes up by 1 permanently, then your energy refills to that maximum. You start with 1 energy on turn one, 2 on turn two, and so on. Unspent energy is lost at end of turn — it does not carry over.

  4. 4

    Main Phase

    Do anything in any order, as many times as you can afford: play cards from your hand, deploy support units to your bench (always free), use your Leader's active ability (once per turn), or trigger a bench unit's active ability. Using a unit's active exhausts it, disabling its passive until your next Recovery Phase.

  5. 5

    Attack Phase

    Your Leader attacks. Choose a target: the opponent's Leader, or one of their bench units. You can also skip the attack entirely.

  6. 6

    End Phase

    Some unit passives trigger here. Turn-based buffs are cleared. The screen passes to your opponent.

Fighting

Combat

Your Leader's damage is shown on their card. This can be increased by support unit passives and equipment cards during your turn.

Attacking the opponent's Leader: deal your effective damage to their HP. Some support unit passives on the defending side can reduce this damage.

Attacking a bench unit: deal your effective damage to that unit's HP. If it reaches zero, the unit is KO'd — it goes to the discard pile and its controller takes 2 damage to their Leader as a penalty.

Blocking

When your Leader is targeted, you may choose one of your non-exhausted bench units to block. The attack damage goes to the blocker instead of your Leader. If the blocker is KO'd, the 2-damage penalty still applies to you. The blocker does not exhaust from blocking. You cannot block if all your bench units are exhausted.

Elemental Shield

Blocks the next damage event directed at your Leader — including KO penalty damage. The shield clears after absorbing one damage event. You play it on yourself; it protects you during your opponent's next turn.

Your bench

Support Units

You can have up to 3 support units on your bench at once. They cost no energy to deploy — just play them from your hand. Each unit has a passive (always active while not exhausted) and an active ability (costs energy, exhausts the unit).

Cards you play

Equipment, Stages, and Genesis Cards

These cards all cost energy to play. Equipment and Genesis cards go to your discard after use. Stages stay on the board until replaced — only one Stage can be active at a time, and playing a new one sends the old one to your discard.

There is a maximum of one Genesis card per deck. You can have zero if you prefer.

Leaders

Leader Cards

Your Leader is placed in the Leader zone before the game starts and is not part of your thirty-card deck. Each Leader has a base HP, a base damage value, and one active ability that costs energy and can be used once per turn.

More Leaders may be added in future updates. Currently available:

Name HP Damage Active (cost)
Sonic the Hedgehog 10 1 Discard 1 card from hand, then draw 2. Requires at least 1 card in hand. (1)

Building your deck

Deck Rules

Exactly 30 cards per deck. Maximum 3 copies of any non-Genesis card. Maximum 1 Genesis card (0 is fine). The deck builder enforces all of this and won't let you start with an invalid deck.

Three prebuilt decks are available if you want to play immediately without building your own.
